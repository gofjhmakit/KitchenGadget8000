## Ingredients
- 1 cup Whole milk
- 1/3 cup Brown sugar
- 2 teaspoons Vanilla extract
- 1.5 teaspoons Cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg
- pinch Salt
- Powdered sugar for dusting
- Fresh berries for topping
- 8 croissants butter croissants
- 8 eggs large eggs
- 1 cup heavy cream
- Maple Syrup

## Instructions
1. Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish generously.
2. Tear croissants into rough 1.5-inch chunks. Scatter them evenly in the baking dish — it's fine to pile them up a bit.
3. Whisk together eggs, heavy cream, milk, brown sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until smooth.
4. Pour the custard mixture evenly over the croissant pieces. Press the chunks down gently with a spatula so they soak up the liquid.
5. Cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight (or at least 4 hours).
6. In the morning: preheat oven to 350°F. Remove the casserole from the fridge and let it sit at room temperature while the oven heats.
7. Bake uncovered for 40–45 minutes until the top is golden brown, puffed up, and the center is set (not jiggly).
8. Let cool 5 minutes. Dust with powdered sugar if you're feeling fancy. Serve with a generous pour of Kirkland maple syrup.
